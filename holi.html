Holi – The Festival of Colors

Celebrated across India and beyond, Holi marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil.

What is Holi?

Holi is a joyful Hindu festival known for its exuberant use of colors, music, and dance. It commemorates the legend of Prahlad and Holika, symbolizing the victory of devotion and righteousness.

Traditions

  • Bonfires on Holika Dahan night to burn away negativity
  • Throwing colored powders and water during Rangwali Holi
  • Sharing sweets like gujiya and thandai
  • Visiting friends and family to spread joy

Significance

Holi is more than just a festival of colors—it's a celebration of unity, renewal, and the joy of life. It brings people together, erasing boundaries and spreading happiness.
