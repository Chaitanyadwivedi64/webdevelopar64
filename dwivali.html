Diwali – The Festival of Lights

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most celebrated festivals in India and among Indian communities worldwide.

What is Diwali?

Diwali symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. It commemorates Lord Rama’s return to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana, and is also associated with Goddess Lakshmi, the deity of wealth and prosperity.

Celebrations

  • Lighting diyas and candles to illuminate homes
  • Decorating with rangoli and flowers
  • Worship of Goddess Lakshmi and Lord Ganesha
  • Exchanging gifts and sweets
  • Bursting fireworks and enjoying festive meals

Significance

Diwali is not just a religious festival—it’s a celebration of hope, renewal, and community. It brings families together and inspires reflection, gratitude, and joy.
